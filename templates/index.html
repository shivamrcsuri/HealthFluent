    Race/Ethnicity

    In a number of industrialized/developed countries across the world, majorities are given first preference when it comes to healthcare, while racial minorities are given the least preference. This often results in low quality service.

    Gender Identity/Sexual Orientation

    According to BMC Public Health, a number of sexual minorities who do not classify as "heterosexual" experience discrimination, stigma, and stress due to a lack of personal, family, and social acceptance.

    Class/Income

    People of low income are not given opportunities for high quality healthcare due to costs and affordability. Additionally, due to the COVID 19 pandemic, many individuals have lost their position in the workforce and are struggling in unemployment. This has also reduced their chances of receiving a satisfactory health insurance and beneficial health care.

    Region

    Many areas are affected more by toxic pollutants and diseases than others, and this may even be within nations. That is why healthcare is offered more in some places than others, and there are many places to seek help that individuals are not educated about.

    Therefore, here at HealthFluent, we aim to change this by offering equal chances for booking appointments regardless of ethnicity, sexual orientation and gender identity, and educating the public of health providers in their region. Our services provide locations that are suitable for the users' income.
